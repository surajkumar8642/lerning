## What interviewer is really asking

When interviewer says:

- your words are overlapping
- we do not know about your project
- I want to know what you understand from your communication

They usually mean:

- you are using too many general words
- you are not explaining one project clearly
- they cannot understand your exact role
- they want concrete communication, not only technical keywords

## What you should do

Always answer in this order:

1. what the project is
2. what problem it solves
3. who uses it
4. what your role was
5. what you personally did
6. what impact your work created

## The Wrong Style

Do not say:

I worked on microservices, cloud, architecture, APIs, performance, DevOps, security, team leadership, scalability, maintainability, and distributed systems.

Why this is weak:

- too many words
- no project context
- sounds generic
- interviewer still does not know what you built

## The Right Style

Say:

I worked on a Hospital Management System for a US client. It was used to manage hospital operations such as patient records, appointments, billing, and third-party integrations. My role was mainly on backend architecture and API development. I designed service communication, built APIs, handled authentication, and improved database performance. That helped improve response time and reduce manual work for hospital staff.

## Speaking Rule

For every answer, use this pattern:

- project
- purpose
- role
- work
- impact

## Avoid These Words Too Much

- scalable
- architecture
- microservices
- robust
- enterprise-grade
- optimized
- maintainable

Use them only after you explain the project clearly.

## Better Words

Use clearer words:

- hospital system
- patient records
- billing
- APIs
- backend
- integrations
- team support
- performance improvement
- reduced manual work
- improved response time

## Final Reminder

The interviewer does not want a list of technologies first.

The interviewer wants:

- what the system does
- what your role was
- what you did
- what changed because of your work
